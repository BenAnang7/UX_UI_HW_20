Non-profit Website Redesign (OMSI)

In this project, our focus was to redesign the layout, color scheme, typography, images, and user interface components to improve user experience, easy navigation and a cleaner and more intuitive layout.

Research Phase

The research methodology used for this project involved conducting a survey that combined both qualitative and quantitative methods. This aimed to bring out insights, including user experiences, opinions, and preferences through open-ended responses, and also quantifiable data that could be analyzed to gather some patterns

Problem Statement

OMSI’s Homepage, Community Programs and Museum Field Trip Pages were designed to help plan community and educational group trips to OMSI. We have observed that various UI elements lack clarity and are overwhelming. Buttons are difficult to locate and engage with and have odd transitions. In addition, the color palette is cluttered and too large and contains over complex gradients. This confuses users and discourage them from engaging with the content effectively, eventually undermining the website’s purpose and functionality.
